That is a mistake. Because the insulation decision you make at construction affects your electricity bill, your indoor temperature, and your AC system sizing for the entire life of the building.

## What glasswool does

Glasswool is made from fine glass fibres bonded into rolls or boards. The fibres trap millions of tiny air pockets. Air is a poor conductor of heat. That is the entire mechanism — and it is extremely effective.

In Indian summers, where roof surface temperatures on metal or concrete can reach 60–70°C, this matters enormously. The two things glasswool controls:

- **Thermal insulation** — reduces heat entering the building through the roof and walls
- **Acoustic insulation** — reduces noise transmission, particularly rain noise on metal roofs

## Where it makes the biggest difference: the roof

The roof is the single largest surface through which heat enters an Indian building. In summer, it receives direct solar radiation for most of the day. An uninsulated roof acts as a radiator into the space below.

Glasswool under-deck insulation on roofs delivers measurable, quantifiable results:

- **AC-cooled buildings:** Properly insulated roofs reduce air conditioning energy consumption by **up to 35%** in Indian climatic conditions. (Source: peer-reviewed building energy simulation studies for Indian composite climate zones; Winrock India independent study showing up to 53% savings in peak summer months.)
- **Non-AC buildings (PEB warehouses, factories):** Glasswool under-deck insulation reduces interior temperature by **6–7°C** during peak summer months compared to an uninsulated metal roof. (Source: Saint-Gobain published data for 100mm glasswool application.)

To put the AC saving in context — if your building spends ₹10 lakh per year on electricity for cooling, correct roof insulation can reduce that by ₹3–3.5 lakh annually. The insulation pays for itself within 2–3 years and delivers savings for the next 25–30 years.

## The PEB application

Pre-engineered buildings — warehouses, factories, logistics centres — are where glasswool is most widely used in India, and where the benefit is most visible.

An uninsulated metal PEB roof in May in Delhi is essentially a steel sheet at 65°C sitting above your workspace. Workers cannot function. Products that are temperature-sensitive cannot be stored. Machinery overheats.

A 50mm glasswool blanket with appropriate facing — typically aluminium FSK or glass tissue — installed as under-deck insulation brings that interior temperature down to workable levels without air conditioning. For AC buildings, it dramatically reduces the chiller and AHU sizing required, cutting both capital cost and running cost.

The specification most commonly used for PEB insulation in India is **24 kg/m³ density, 50mm thickness**, conforming to IS 8183. For higher thermal performance, 48 kg/m³ or greater thickness is specified.

## Beyond thermal: what else glasswool does

- **Fire safety:** Glasswool is non-combustible. It conforms to Class P (BS 476 Part 5) and Class 1 fire ratings.
- **Acoustic comfort:** A metal roof in heavy rain can reach 78 dB(A). Glasswool brings this below 55 dB(A), the ISO 9241 workplace threshold.
- **Condensation control:** In humid Indian climates, uninsulated metal roofs develop condensation. Glasswool, correctly installed with appropriate vapour barriers, prevents moisture damage and corrosion.

## What to specify

When asking for glasswool in a tender or procurement:

- State the density (kg/m³) and thickness (mm) explicitly
- Call out IS 8183 conformance
- Specify the facing required — FSK for reflective thermal performance, glass tissue for acoustic applications
- Ask for test certificates
