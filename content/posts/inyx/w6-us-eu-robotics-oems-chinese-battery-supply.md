The electronics and battery supply chain disruptions of 2020–2022 did more to accelerate supply chain diversification than a decade of risk management frameworks. For US and European robotics OEMs, the single most acute exposure was battery packs — specifically lithium cells and pack assemblies sourced from Chinese manufacturers who dominated the supply chain by volume, cost, and vertical integration.

At [iNYX Batteries](https://www.inyxbatteries.com), we supply LFP battery packs with Lithion Power BMS to robotics OEMs in the US, Europe, and Japan. The enquiries we receive have shifted qualitatively over the past 18 months — from exploratory conversations about Indian manufacturing capability to active qualification programmes driven by procurement mandates to diversify away from single-country cell and pack sourcing.

## What changed the conversation

**The 2020–2022 disruptions** exposed the fragility of single-source battery supply chains in ways that risk models had not adequately captured. Lead times for lithium cells from Chinese manufacturers extended from 8–12 weeks to 40+ weeks. Pack assembly slots were allocated to domestic Chinese customers first. OEMs with no alternative supply relationships had no leverage and no options.

**The US regulatory environment** has hardened significantly. The CHIPS and Science Act contains provisions that affect procurement decisions for companies receiving government grants or working on defence-adjacent programmes. The Inflation Reduction Act's domestic content requirements for battery components in certain applications create direct financial incentives for sourcing outside China. For US robotics OEMs with government contracts or IRA-eligible products, Chinese-sourced battery packs are increasingly a compliance issue, not just a supply risk.

**EU battery regulation** — specifically the EU Battery Regulation (2023/1542) which came into force in 2023 — introduces due diligence requirements for battery supply chains that include geographic sourcing disclosure, carbon footprint reporting, and traceability requirements. For European OEMs, the administrative burden of demonstrating Chinese supply chain compliance with these requirements is generating pressure to source from regions with cleaner documentation trails.

**Japan's supply chain resilience push** following the same 2020–2022 disruption period has driven Japanese electronics and robotics manufacturers to actively seek non-Chinese supply for critical components. The Japanese government's economic security legislation explicitly identifies battery supply chains as a strategic priority.

## Why China remains dominant — and what that means for alternatives

It would be inaccurate to suggest that Chinese battery manufacturing is being displaced. China produces approximately 75–80% of global lithium battery cells and an even higher proportion of assembled packs. The cost, scale, and vertical integration advantages of Chinese manufacturers are structural and will persist for the foreseeable future.

What has changed is the risk calculus. OEMs are no longer asking "should we source 100% from China?" — they are asking "what percentage of our battery supply can we source from qualified alternatives, and at what cost premium?" The target diversification most US and European OEMs are working toward is 20–40% of volume from non-Chinese sources, not a complete switch.

This is the market that Indian LFP manufacturing is positioned to serve.

## What Indian LFP manufacturing offers

**Comparable cell quality at competitive cost** — Indian LFP cell manufacturers have closed the quality gap with Chinese tier-2 cell producers. For AMR and drone applications where the performance requirements are demanding but not at the frontier of energy density (where Chinese tier-1 manufacturers still hold the advantage), Indian-manufactured LFP cells meet specification.

**Customisation turnaround** — Chinese pack manufacturers serving export markets operate at high volume with standardised configurations. Custom pack geometries, connector specifications, and BMS communication protocols are accommodated, but with lead times and minimum order quantities that disadvantage smaller OEMs. iNYX operates with shorter customisation lead times and lower MOQs — relevant for robotics OEMs whose pack requirements are application-specific.

**Tariff exposure** — US Section 301 tariffs on Chinese-manufactured battery packs range from 7.5% to 25% depending on classification. For OEMs sensitive to landed cost, Indian-manufactured packs with preferential trade treatment under existing frameworks can be cost-competitive on a tariff-adjusted basis even against nominally lower Chinese ex-works prices.

**English-language technical support and firmware access** — this is the differentiator that comes up consistently in qualification conversations. Chinese BMS manufacturers serving the export market provide English-language datasheets and standard communication protocol documentation. What they typically do not provide is firmware-level access to modify protection thresholds, add custom fault codes, or integrate with proprietary robot communication architectures.

Lithion Power BMS — the BMS in every iNYX pack — is designed and manufactured in India. Lithion Power's application engineering team works directly with OEM engineering teams on firmware customisation. This is the same team that built the BMS, not a third-party integrator translating between the OEM and an overseas manufacturer.

## The qualification process

For US and European OEMs qualifying iNYX packs as an alternative supply source, the qualification process typically covers:

- Cell specification and lot traceability documentation
- Pack mechanical drawing and dimensional verification
- BMS communication protocol documentation and integration testing
- Environmental testing: vibration (IEC 60068-2-6), thermal cycling, humidity
- Electrical characterisation: capacity, internal resistance, cycle life at rated conditions
- Safety testing: overcharge, over-discharge, external short circuit, crush (UN 38.3)

iNYX maintains a qualification dossier for each standard pack variant, covering all standard tests. For non-standard configurations, we provide application engineering support through the qualification programme.
